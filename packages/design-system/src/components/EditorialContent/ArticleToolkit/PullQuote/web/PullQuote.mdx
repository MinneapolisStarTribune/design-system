# Pull Quote

The **Pull Quote** component highlights a key excerpt from an article to draw reader attention and emphasize important statements or quotes.
It is designed specifically for editorial content and is supported **only in the immersive article variant**, where it enhances storytelling by breaking the flow of text with visually distinct emphasis.

[Figma Comps](https://www.figma.com/design/f8DSKwKjG4MsSqmA59v1um/Core-Components?node-id=2394-12749)

---

## Anatomy

1. **Quote** — The excerpt pulled directly from the article body.
2. **Quote-mark** — Visual indicator reinforcing that the content is quoted.
3. **Attribute** — Includes the quoted individual’s name and typically their title or role, as provided by the newsroom.

---

## Variants

### Standard

Pull quotes are **not supported** in the standard article layout.

### Immersive

Used within the immersive article template to emphasize key statements and improve visual storytelling.

---

## Sizes

- **Small** (`small`) — Compact quote style suitable for shorter excerpts.
- **Large** (`large`) — More prominent quote style for longer or more impactful statements.

---

## Usage guidelines

To use the component in consuming applications:

```tsx
import { PullQuote } from '@minneapolisstartribune/design-system/web';
```

### When to use

- Highlight impactful or important quotes within an article.
- Break long-form content with visual emphasis.
- Provide attribution to quoted individuals when relevant.

---

### When to avoid

- Avoid using in the **standard article variant**.
- Avoid overusing — too many pull quotes reduce their impact.

---

## Behavior

- The component **does not render** if no `quote` is provided.
- In the **standard variant**, a fallback message is displayed indicating that pull quotes are not supported.
- The icon size and layout are responsive and adapt across breakpoints using design tokens.

---

## Props

- **quote** – The main quote text to display.
- **attribution** – Optional name of the person being quoted.
- **jobTitle** – Optional job title or descriptor for the attribution.
- **size** – Controls quote styling: `'small' | 'large'`.
- **variant** – Article variant: `'standard' | 'immersive'`.
- **className** – Optional custom class for styling overrides.
- **dataTestId** – Optional data-testid attribute for testing.

---

## Layout behavior

- The component aligns with the editorial grid and adjusts spacing responsively.
- In immersive layouts, the quote icon may offset slightly to align with the article column structure.
- Maximum width is controlled via design tokens to maintain readability.
